# CS250-Sprint-Review-and-Retrospective

How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? 
I interprest user needs by gathering requirements then use those requirements to create user stories at a high level first. I implement them by collaborating with the tester in the development team to define acceptance criteria. The criteria have to meet what the high level requirements are expecting. The acceptance tests ensure the functionality meets the user stories that are written from the end user's perspective.

How does creating “user stories” help with this?
Creating user stories helps ensure the team knows what to do and accomplish during scrum activities. The sprints have concrete goals to meet and they're actionable. The user stories get refined with the tester and the developer knows what the results should be. 

How do I approach developing programs? 
I approach developing programs by first defining requirements. Interacting with the client often and regularly receiving feedback. The feedback needs to be communicated regularly with the agile team. The main piece is effective communciation with organizational tools like Jira or Click Up.

What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?
An agile process I want to incorporate into my future development work is more TDD, test driven development. I think working in mind with what needs to pass using unit tests would ensure functionality. The unit tests can be run per release and identify what changes affect future development. 

What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?
I think a good team meber in software development is someone that knows how to communicate concerns and ask questions without hestitation. They are also someone that checks in with what pieces they complete or what areas are taking longer so that another team member can jump in for support. A good team member speaks up when they identify a gap in development and offer solutions when they are an expert in a specific area.
